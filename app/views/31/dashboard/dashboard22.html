Your State Pension

You’ll reach State Pension age on 1 December 2017 when you’ll be 66

It’s not possible for you to get any State Pension based on your National Insurance record

You need at least 10 years of contributions to get any State Pension and you do not have enough time to get these.

  • you have 6 years of full National Insurance contibitions on your record up to 5 April 2016
  • after State Pension age in a year, you no longer pay National Insurance contributions

What you can do next

  • check your National Insurance record, you may be able to make gaps in your record toward towards your pension
  • if you have lived or worked overseas (opens in new tab), you may be able to use your time abroad to make your years in the UK qualify for the purposes of getting any State Pension
  • You may be eligible for Pension Credit (opens in new tab) if your retirement income is low

What else you can do

Contact the Pensions Advisory Service (opens in new tab) for free impartial advice.
