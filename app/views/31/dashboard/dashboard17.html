Your State Pension

You’ll reach State Pension age on 1 December 2020 when you’ll be 67

If you continue contributing, you’ll be 3 years short of getting any State Pension.

You will not get any State Pension based on your National Insurance record.

What you can do next

If you have lived or worked overseas (opens in new tab), those years may count towards the 10 years you need to get any State Pension.

You also have 2 years on your record which do not count towards your pension because you did not contribute enough National Insurance.

Gaps in your record and the cost of filling them

After State Pension age, 1 December 2020 you no longer pay National Insurance contributions.

What else you can do

You may have a private pension. Use the free service to find pension contact details (opens in new tab).

You may be eligible for Pension Credit (opens in new tab) if your retirement income is low.

Contact the Pensions Advisory Service (opens in new tab) for free impartial advice.
