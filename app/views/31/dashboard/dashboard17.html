Your State Pension

You’ll reach State Pension age on 1 December 2020 when you’ll be 67.

By this time, you will not be able to get the 10 years needed on your National Insurance record to get any State Pension.

What you can do next

If you have lived or worked overseas (opens in new tab), you may be able to use your time abroad to make your years in the UK qualify for the purposes of getting any State Pension.

You also have 2 years on your record which do not count towards your pension because you did not contribute enough National Insurance. Filling these are not enough to get any State Pension.

View your National Insurance record

After State Pension age, 1 December 2020 you no longer pay National Insurance contributions.

What else you can do

You may have a private pension. Use the free service to find pension contact details (opens in new tab).

You may be eligible for Pension Credit (opens in new tab) if your retirement income is low.

Contact the Pensions Advisory Service (opens in new tab) for free impartial advice.
