Paying voluntary contributions

You can pay voluntary contributions to fill gaps in your National Insurance record to make years count towards your State Pension.

Paying through your bank or building society

The quickest and easiest way to make a payment is using internet or telephone banking. You can also call into your local branch.

  • Account name: HMRC
  • Account number: 123456789
  • Sort code: 12-34-56

Your reference is ABC123DOREMI

Payment by cheque

You can send your payment to HMRC directly. Make your cheque payable to ‘HM Revenue and Customs only’ followed by your reference number.

Include a note with:

  • your name, address and telephone number
  • your reference number or National Insurance number
  • the periods you’re paying for
  • whether you want a receipt

Post it to:

HM Revenue and Customs
National Insurance Contributions and Employer Office
Benton Park View
Newcastle upon Tyne
NE98 1ZZ

Allow 3 working days for the payment to reach HMRC.
