Your State Pension

You'll reach State Pension age on 1 December 2020 when you'll be 66

You currently have 3 years on your National Insurance record, using the latest complete tax year, up to 5 April 2014. You need at least 10 years to get any State Pension.

It may be possible for you to get some State Pension

You need to add 7 years to your record and you have 4 years left to contribute before you reach State Pension age on 1 December 2020.

You also have 5 years on your record which don't count towards your pension because you didn't contribute enough National Insurance. You can fill some of these years to get the 10 years you need to get any State Pension.

After State Pension age, 1 December 2020 you no longer pay National Insurance contributions.

View your National Insurance record.

If you have lived or worked overseas, you may be able to use your time abroad to make your years in the UK qualify for the purposes of getting any State Pension. More information on living and working overseas (opens in new tab).
