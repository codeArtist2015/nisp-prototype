Check and correct your National Insurance record

If you think your details are wrong you may be able to correct them. If you were unemployed or unable to work you may be entitled to claim National Insurance credits. Correcting your record or claiming credits may make years count towards your State Pension.

How to check and correct your record

Paid employment
Check the contributions in your record against P60s from your employers.

Self-employment and voluntary contributions
Check the contributions in your record against your personal accounts, for instance if you made payments by cheque or through your bank.

If you think your details are wrong you can download a form and return it with your evidence.

National Insurance credits

If you were claiming benefits because you were unable to work, unemployed or caring for someone full time, these should have been credited automatically. If you think you were entitled to credits that aren't on your record, check eligibility for credits and find out how to get them.
