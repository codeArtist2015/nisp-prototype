Paying voluntary contributions

Before paying voluntary contributions, as well as checking your National Insurance record is correct, you should:

1.Seek guidance or financial advice

Paying voluntary contributions may not be your best option when planning for your retirement.

Where you can get guidance and advice

You can seek professional financial advice. For free impartial guidance (not on GOV.UK)

  • Money Advice Service (opens in new tab)
  • Citizens Advice (opens in new tab)

2.Check if you may get extra pension income because you

inherit or increase it from a partner

Your amount may change if you:

  • are widowed, divorced or have dissolved your civil partnership
  • paid married women’s reduced rate contributions
lived or worked overseas

    You may be entitled to a State Pension from the country you lived or worked in. Contact the pension service of the country you were in to find out if you are eligible.
